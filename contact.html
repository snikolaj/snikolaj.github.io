My main project at the moment

ProgramKitsForEveryone

How it all started

One day I was on the KDE subreddit and found an interesting link. It was to a competition organized by the GNOME foundation. One thing led to another, and a few months later I was declared one of the 20 winners of the first phase.

The idea

My idea was inspired by something interesting I saw on the internet a while ago. I was interested in retro computers, and found out that before sharing programs on storage media was common, people had to read programs from magazines and write them out themselves, thereby learning programming. My idea was a reimagining of that, but using modern free and open source programs, so that anyone can access and build with these programs.

The outreach

What made me very happy and motivated was the response in my school to my project. I tried a similar version of the project even before the comptetition, but this really made me passionate about creating and helping other people learn programming. Not only did my friends love the idea, but my school decided to dedicate an entire class every Wednesday to teaching beginners programming.

Fun fact: I used Edubuntu with GNOME in primary school way before I even knew what Linux or GNOME was. It feels so amazing to work with a company whose product I used extensively for such a long time.
